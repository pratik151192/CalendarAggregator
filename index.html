Describe your prototype (WHAT we aim to achieve, HOW we plan to achieve our vision, HOW we differentiate from our competition)

  • What is your prototype and how does it fulfill your purpose (or solve the problem that you pose)

      • A passive event aggregation system will collate calendars from various systems in sites (especially .ics format), and have a single aggregated calendar showing events from the University, clubs, city, and social networks. No additional effort from the student will be necessary. This system is mocked up on this website.

      • An active life management system would place course schedule on a personal calendar, find free time, and populate it with events and deadlines.

      • Email Mailing Lists - Currently, most students obtain their event information through disparate mailing lists. However, these are often restricted to clubs and majors, and the flood of emails often means many people simply gloss over email newsletters.

      • PSU Major Websites - Penn State is littered with failed attempts to build a event organizing system, and the ones that have succeeded are generally limited to a major. The systems that have stood up are the blogs, the social networks, and the emails, but these have little calendar integration.
